Description

We hope this message finds you in the best of imaan and health. Alhamdulillaah, the Muslim population is growing in the Irving area and there is a dire need for traditional Islamic education in an Islamic environment. The Hadeeth states that “It is fard (compulsory) for every Muslim to gain knowledge” (Ibn Maajah). Due to our busy schedules, women are unable to go outside and seek knowledge. Memorizing the Quran and Ahadeeth are just the beginning of acquiring knowledge about Islam. Mashaa’Allah there are many Hifz programs in the Irving area, however, there are not many ‘Aalimiyyah programs which would lead the huffaz towards their next step in gaining Islamic knowledge; understanding the Quran and Ahadeeth. There are NO places in the Irving area that are catering to this demand. Because of the lack of said program, many young students travel outside of Texas and even outside of country seeking to understand the Quran and Sunnah.

By the grace of Allah SWT, Darul Hikmah is pleased to begin an ‘Aalimiyyah Preliminary Course for sisters of all ages who would be either interested in joining our future Full-Time ‘Aalimiyyah Program or motivated in gaining some in-depth Islamic knowledge. We have created a program which makes it easier for the sisters in the community to learn more about Islam in a close-knit environment. The program is developed in such a way that even beginners will be able to grasp the topics quickly. Fus’hah Arabic is the main focus in this preliminary course to make the transition easier for students who choose to continue to the Full-Time ‘Aalimiyyah Course, and it will be taught daily while the other subjects will alternate between the days. Although this is a preliminary course, it is design for students to gain enough knowledge to understand several aayaat (verses), phrases, and short suras from the Quran Inshaa’Allah if they wish not to continue on to Full-Time Aalimiyyah Program.

Please read below for more information regarding this program:

Program Outline

Code Subject Description
ARR 101 Arabic Reading I Oral Reading and applying Nahw and Sarf in the course. Grammatical Analysis of sentences. Application of I’raab/Harakaat to sentences.
ARW 100 Arabic Writing I Writing Arabic letters/alphabets and learning how to connect these letters to form words.
ARW 101 Arabic Writing II Learning to form sentences and application of Nahw and Sarf to the writing process.
ARG 101 Arabic Grammar I Syntax-Nahw consists of parts of speech and analyzing sentences.Etymology-Sarf consists of verbs and conjugation of verbs.
ARS 101 Arabic Speaking & Listening I Learning to apply Nahw and Sarf to speech by practicing speaking in Arabic.
SER 101 Seerah I Translating, understanding and learning morals from the lives of the prophets.
FQH 101 Fiqh (Hanafi) I Islamic Jurisprudence (Optional)
ETH 100 Islamic Etiquettes I Memorization of Masnoon Azhkaar and Salaah Ad’iyaa (Optional)
ETH 101 Islamic Etiquettes II Memorization of 99 names of Allah with meaning & 40 Ahaadeeth (Optional)


After successful completion of this program you will learn:

  • Read Arabic texts without I’raab/Harakaat (beginner’s level).
  • Write Arabic sentences independently.
  • Translate many Aayaat and Ahaadeeth on their own.
  • Speak and Understand Fus’hah Arabic.
  • Comprehend Arabic conversation at a greater level by Listening.
  • Morph Arabic verbs on their own.
  • Analyze short Arabic sentences.
  • Retain over 100 new Quranic vocabulary words.
  • Develop a strong connection with Quran and Ahadeeth.
  • Learn all Fiqhi principles of Tahaarah (physical and legal impurities).
  • Learn all Fiqhi principles of Salaah.
  • Gain more khushu’ in salaah.
  • Retain all names of Allah memorized during the course with their meaning.
  • Retain the Ahaadeth memorized during the course.
  • Retain the Ad’iyaa memorized during the course.
  • Develop stronger Islamic etiquettes.
  • Attain enough skill to make transition to more rigorous Islamic studies easier.

Prerequisite for Enrollment:

  • The student must be able to read Quran fluently.
  • The student must be able to pronounce the Arabic alphabets from their point of articulation.
  • The student must know basic Tajweed.
  • The student must be able to connect Arabic letters in writing.
  • The student must know the basics of wudhu and salaah, and she must be able to perform them without the supervision of an adult.
  • Each student will be interviewed

Age Requirement:

  • High school and college students who are too busy to take full-time Islamic courses.
